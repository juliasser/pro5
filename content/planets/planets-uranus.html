Ice all over!

Uranus is pretty similiar to Neptune. Both being two of the four gas giants in our solar system, they're both ice giants because of their special primary composition, containing more "ices" like water, ammonia and methane.

That's right: Uranus is basically rocks and ices, layered with even more ice, additionally winning first place in the "how cold can it get"-contest of the solar system. Its atmosphere takes the cake as the coldest planetary atmosphere in the solar system, with a minimum temperature of -224.2°C. That's pretty cold, right?

It's huge!

Uranus is the third largest planet in the whole solar system — the earth would fit inside the gas giant about 64 times.

Ring system

Even though it's not as distinctive as Saturn's, Uranus has a ring system which is actually quite similiar. In 1977, the ring system was accidentally discovered and first imaged by VOYAGER 2 in 1986.
